# Triple-L-Standard-v0.1

### An Open Standard for Lifelong Learner Identification and Documentation

The **Triple L (Life Long Learner) Standard** is an open specification for uniquely identifying learners and documenting lifelong learning across learning communities.

It is designed to support schools, homeschooling communities, micro-schools, alternative education initiatives, research projects and other learning ecosystems through a common, open and interoperable standard.

The Triple L Standard is one component of the broader **Lifelong Learning Framework**, an open initiative exploring new ways of understanding, documenting and supporting human learning throughout life.

---

## Why?

Most learner identification systems belong to institutions.

When a learner changes schools, organisations or learning communities, their learning history often becomes fragmented.

The Triple L Standard proposes a learner-centred approach where the identifier belongs to the learner rather than to any single institution.

---

## Design Principles

* Learner-centred
* Open and community-driven
* Human-readable
* Privacy-conscious
* Software-independent
* Based on existing international standards wherever possible
* Designed for lifelong learning

## Status

This project is currently in active development.

The first reference implementation is being piloted by **Walking Ferns** through its Kuttinaadu learning community in Kerala, India.

Feedback, discussion and contributions are welcome.
